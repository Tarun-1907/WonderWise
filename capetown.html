Top 5 Things to Do

1. Table Mountain

Iconic flat-topped mountain with panoramic views. Hike up Platteklip Gorge (2-3 hours) or take the cable car.

Cable car cost: R390 (~$21) roundtrip

Best Time: Early morning to avoid clouds and crowds

2. Cape Peninsula Tour

Drive along Chapman's Peak to Cape Point, stopping at Boulders Beach's penguin colony.

Cost: R85 (~$4.60) park entry + transport

Best Time: Full day trip starting early

3. Robben Island

UNESCO site where Nelson Mandela was imprisoned. Tours led by former prisoners.

Cost: R600 (~$32) for ferry and tour

Best Time: Morning tours (weather dependent)

4. Kirstenbosch Gardens

Magnificent botanical gardens at the foot of Table Mountain with treetop walkway.

Cost: R220 (~$12) entry

Best Time: Summer sunset concerts (Dec-Mar)

5. Winelands Day Trip

Visit Stellenbosch and Franschhoek for world-class wines and stunning scenery.

Cost: R500-1000 (~$27-54) for tours

Best Time: Weekdays to avoid crowds

Must-Try Cape Town Food

Braai (BBQ)

South Africa's famous barbecue with boerewors sausage and sosatie skewers.

Where to try: Mzoli's in Gugulethu (authentic township experience)

Price: R100-200 (~$5-11) per person

Cape Malay Curry

Fragrant, mildly spiced curry reflecting the city's multicultural heritage.

Where to try: Bo-Kaap Kombuis with Table Mountain views

Price: R150-250 (~$8-14) main course

Bunny Chow

Hollowed-out bread loaf filled with curry, originally from Durban but popular in Cape Town.

Where to try: Eastern Food Bazaar (budget-friendly)

Price: R60-120 (~$3-6.50)

Budget Tips

Use the City Pass

Cape Town City Pass includes Table Mountain cableway, hop-on-hop-off bus, and other attractions.

Take Uber or MyCiTi Bus

Much cheaper than taxis. MyCiTi bus day pass is R110 (~$6).

Free Walking Tours

Excellent "pay-what-you-want" tours of city center and Bo-Kaap.

Self-Cater Accommodation

Airbnbs with kitchens let you save by preparing some meals yourself.

Sample 5-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival & V&A Waterfront

  • Morning: Arrive, check into accommodation
  • Afternoon: Explore V&A Waterfront and Greenmarket Square
  • Evening: Sunset at Signal Hill

Day 2: Table Mountain & City Tour

  • Early morning: Table Mountain (cable car or hike)
  • Afternoon: Free walking tour of city center and Company's Garden
  • Evening: Dinner in Kloof Street area

Day 3: Cape Peninsula

  • Full day: Chapman's Peak Drive, Cape Point, Boulders Beach
  • Stop in Simon's Town for lunch
  • Return via Kommetjie for sunset

Day 4: Robben Island & Bo-Kaap

  • Morning: Robben Island tour
  • Afternoon: Colorful Bo-Kaap neighborhood and Cape Malay cuisine
  • Evening: Kirstenbosch sunset concert (summer only)

Day 5: Winelands or Beach Day

  • Option 1: Stellenbosch/Franschhoek wine tour
  • Option 2: Relax at Camps Bay or Clifton beaches
  • Evening: Farewell braai (BBQ)

Essential Information

Visa Requirements

Many nationalities get 90 days visa-free. Check requirements based on your passport.

Language

English widely spoken along with Afrikaans and Xhosa. Basic greetings in local languages appreciated.

FAQs

Is Cape Town safe for tourists?

Generally yes in tourist areas, but take normal precautions. Avoid displaying valuables and walking alone at night in certain areas.

When is the best time to visit?

November-March (summer) for beach weather, but most crowded. April-May and September-October offer pleasant weather with fewer tourists.

Do I need to tip in Cape Town?

Yes, 10-15% at restaurants is standard. Round up taxi fares. Tip tour guides R50-100 (~$3-5) for good service.

What should I pack?

Layers for changeable weather, good walking shoes, swimwear, sunscreen, adapter, and a warm jacket for evenings even in summer.
