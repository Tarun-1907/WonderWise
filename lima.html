Lima, Peru

Gastronomic Capital
20°C, Cloudy
Budget: $
Timezone: GMT-5

Gateway to Machu Picchu with incredible seafood and colonial architecture.

Top 5 Things to Do

1. Miraflores District

Upscale coastal neighborhood with parks, shopping, and paragliding over cliffs.

Paragliding: ~$60 for 10-minute flight

Best Time: Late afternoon for sunset views

2. Historic Center of Lima

UNESCO World Heritage site with stunning colonial architecture and churches.

Cost: Free to walk around (small fees for churches)

Tip: Join free walking tour at 10am or 3pm

3. Larco Museum

World-class museum of pre-Columbian art with beautiful gardens.

Cost: 35 PEN (~$9)

Best Time: Weekday mornings to avoid crowds

4. Barranco District

Bohemian arts district with colorful streets, galleries, and nightlife.

Cost: Free to explore

Best Time: Evening for sunset at Bridge of Sighs

5. Parque del Amor

Romantic park in Miraflores with mosaic walls and ocean views.

Cost: Free

Best Time: Sunset for stunning colors

Must-Try Peruvian Food

Ceviche

Peru's national dish - fresh raw fish cured in citrus juices with onion and chili.

Where to try: La Mar or El Mercado

Price: 35-50 PEN (~$9-13)

Lomo Saltado

Stir-fried beef with onions, tomatoes, french fries and rice - Peruvian-Chinese fusion.

Where to try: Isolina or Tanta

Price: 30-45 PEN (~$8-12)

Pisco Sour

Peru's signature cocktail made with pisco brandy, lime, egg white and bitters.

Where to try: Museo del Pisco or Ayahuasca Restobar

Price: 25-35 PEN (~$6-9)

Budget Tips

Use Public Transportation

Metropolitano bus system is efficient and costs just 2.50 PEN (~$0.65) per ride.

Eat at Local Markets

Mercado de Surquillo offers amazing seafood at half restaurant prices.

Free Walking Tours

Several companies offer pay-what-you-want tours of historic center.

Stay in Barranco

More affordable than Miraflores but still safe and charming.

Day 1: Historic Lima

Essential Information

Visa Requirements

Many nationalities get 90-180 days visa-free. Check requirements based on passport.

Language

Spanish. English spoken in tourist areas but basic Spanish helpful.

Currency

Peruvian Sol (PEN). $1 ≈ 3.70 PEN. US dollars accepted in many places.

Electricity

220V, 60Hz. Type A/C plugs. US devices may need adapter.

Transportation

Taxis are affordable (use Uber or official taxis). Metropolitano bus for longer distances.

Health & Safety

Drink bottled water. Avoid tap water. Emergency number: 105 (police).

FAQs

Is Lima safe for tourists?

Generally yes in Miraflores, Barranco and San Isidro. Use normal precautions.

When is the best time to visit?

December-April for summer weather. May-November is cooler and foggy but dry.

Do I need altitude medication?

No - Lima is at sea level. Save altitude concerns for Cusco/Machu Picchu.

Can I drink the tap water?

No - stick to bottled water and avoid ice in drinks unless at nice restaurants.
