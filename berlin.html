Berlin, Germany

Historic Capital
10°C, Rainy
Budget: $$
Timezone: GMT+1

Cool, creative, and packed with history, Berlin offers world-class museums and vibrant nightlife.

Top 5 Things to Do

1. Brandenburg Gate

Iconic neoclassical monument symbolizing German reunification. Visit at night when illuminated.

Cost: Free

Best Time: Early morning or evening to avoid crowds

2. Berlin Wall Memorial & East Side Gallery

See remaining sections of the Wall with powerful murals at the world's largest open-air gallery.

Cost: Free (donations welcome)

Best Time: Weekday mornings

3. Museum Island

UNESCO site with five world-class museums including the Pergamon and Neues Museum.

Cost: €19 for day pass to all museums

Best Time: Thursday evenings (longer hours)

4. Reichstag Dome

Glass dome atop the German parliament with 360° city views. Requires advance registration.

Cost: Free (registration required)

Best Time: Sunset for spectacular views

5. Tempelhofer Feld

Massive former airport turned public park where locals picnic, bike, and kite-surf on runways.

Cost: Free

Best Time: Summer evenings

Must-Try Berlin Food

Currywurst

Berlin's iconic street food - steamed then fried pork sausage with curry ketchup and fries.

Where to try: Konnopke's Imbiss (under U-Bahn tracks)

Price: €3-5

Döner Kebab

Berlin's version of this Turkish staple features fresh salads and sauces in fluffy bread.

Where to try: Mustafa's Gemüse Kebap (expect queues)

Price: €4-6

Berliner Pfannkuchen

Jelly-filled doughnuts (no relation to pancakes) - try the original at Bäckerei Siebert.

Where to try: Local bakeries throughout the city

Price: €1-2 each

Budget Tips

Get a WelcomeCard

Includes unlimited public transport and discounts to 200+ attractions (€23 for 48 hours).

Free Walking Tours

Excellent "pay-what-you-want" tours cover major sites. Tip €10-15 if you enjoy it.

Museum Sundays

Many state museums offer free entry on first Sunday of each month.

Eat Street Food

Skip restaurants for affordable and delicious street food markets like Markthalle Neun.

Sample 4-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Historic Berlin

  • Morning: Brandenburg Gate & Reichstag Dome
  • Afternoon: Holocaust Memorial & Checkpoint Charlie
  • Evening: Dinner in Mitte district

Day 2: Cold War Berlin

  • Morning: Berlin Wall Memorial & Documentation Center
  • Afternoon: East Side Gallery & DDR Museum
  • Evening: Drinks in hip Friedrichshain

Day 3: Museum & Culture Day

  • Morning: Museum Island (pick 2-3 museums)
  • Afternoon: Berlin Cathedral & TV Tower
  • Evening: Classical concert at Philharmonie

Day 4: Alternative Berlin

  • Morning: Street art tour in Kreuzberg
  • Afternoon: Tempelhofer Feld & Turkish Market
  • Evening: Nightlife in Berghain area (if you dare)

Essential Information

Visa Requirements

Visa-free for most Western nationalities for up to 90 days. Check requirements based on your passport.

Language

German is official language but English widely spoken, especially by younger generations.

Currency

Euro (€). Credit cards widely accepted but carry some cash for small purchases.

Electricity

230V, 50Hz. Type F plugs (two round pins). Bring adapter if needed.

Transportation

Excellent U-Bahn (subway), S-Bahn, buses. Buy day passes for unlimited travel (€8.80).

Health & Safety

Very safe city. Tap water drinkable. Emergency number: 112. Watch for bikes when walking!

FAQs

Is Berlin expensive to visit?

Compared to other European capitals, Berlin is quite affordable. Budget travelers can manage on €50-70/day, mid-range €80-120.

What's the best way to get from the airport to city center?

From BER Airport, take the FEX train (€3.80) to Hauptbahnhof in 30 minutes. Taxis cost €40-50.

Is the Berlin nightlife really that good?

Yes! Berlin has legendary clubs open all weekend (Berghain, Tresor, etc.). But dress casually - no fancy club attire.

Do I need to speak German?

Not necessary for tourism, but learning basic phrases like "Danke" (thank you) is appreciated.
