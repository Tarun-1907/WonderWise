Mexico City, Mexico

Foodie Paradise
22°C, Sunny
Budget: $
Timezone: GMT-5

Vibrant culture, incredible food, and rich history at budget-friendly prices.

Top 5 Things to Do

1. Zócalo (Plaza de la Constitución)

Massive central square surrounded by historic buildings including the Metropolitan Cathedral.

Cost: Free

Best Time: Morning before crowds arrive

2. Teotihuacán

Ancient Mesoamerican city with massive pyramids located just outside Mexico City.

Cost: 80 MXN (~$4.50) entry

Tip: Arrive early to climb the Pyramid of the Sun before it gets hot

3. Frida Kahlo Museum (Casa Azul)

The iconic blue house where Frida Kahlo lived and worked, now a museum of her life and art.

Cost: 250 MXN (~$14) - book tickets online in advance

Best Time: Weekday afternoons

4. Chapultepec Park & Castle

One of the largest city parks in the world with a castle offering panoramic views.

Cost: 85 MXN (~$4.80) for castle entry

Best Time: Tuesday-Sunday (castle closed Mondays)

5. Xochimilco Canals

Colorful floating gardens where you can ride traditional trajineras (boats) with mariachi music.

Cost: 500 MXN (~$28) per boat (split among group)

Best Time: Weekend afternoons for liveliest atmosphere

Must-Try Mexican Food

Tacos al Pastor

Marinated pork cooked on a vertical spit, served with pineapple on small corn tortillas.

Where to try: El Huequito or Los Cocuyos

Price: 10-15 MXN (~$0.50-0.80) per taco

Chilaquiles

Fried tortilla chips bathed in green or red salsa, topped with cream, cheese and eggs.

Where to try: Lardo or Café Avellaneda

Price: 80-120 MXN (~$4.50-7) for breakfast

Mole

Complex sauce with dozens of ingredients including chocolate, served over chicken.

Where to try: Azul Histórico or Pujol (high-end)

Price: 150-300 MXN (~$8.50-17)

Budget Tips

Use the Metro

Extensive and safe subway system costs just 5 MXN (~$0.30) per ride.

Eat at Markets

Local markets like Mercado de San Juan offer amazing food at half restaurant prices.

Free Museums

Many museums are free on Sundays (including Anthropology Museum).

Stay in Roma/Condesa

These trendy neighborhoods offer great value compared to Polanco.

Essential Information

Visa Requirements

Many nationalities get 180 days visa-free. Check requirements based on your passport.

Language

Spanish. English spoken in tourist areas but basic Spanish phrases helpful.

Currency

Mexican Peso (MXN). $1 ≈ 17-18 MXN. Carry small bills for markets/taxis.

Electricity

127V, 60Hz. Type A/B plugs (same as US). No adapter needed for US devices.

Transportation

Extensive metro (5 MXN/ride), Uber is safe and affordable, or use official taxis.

Health & Safety

Stick to bottled water. Be cautious with street food at first. Emergency number: 911.

FAQs

Is Mexico City safe for tourists?

Generally yes in tourist areas. Use normal precautions like avoiding flashing valuables.

When is the best time to visit?

March-May for pleasant weather. Rainy season is June-October.

Do I need to speak Spanish?

Helpful but not essential in tourist areas. Learn basic phrases like "gracias" (thank you).

Is the tap water safe?

No - stick to bottled water and avoid ice in drinks unless at nice restaurants.
