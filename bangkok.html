Bangkok, Thailand

Capital City

Budget: $
Timezone: GMT+7

Bangkok is a bustling metropolis known for its vibrant street life, ornate temples, and incredible food scene.

Top 5 Things to Do

1. Grand Palace & Wat Phra Kaew

Bangkok's most famous landmark, this stunning complex houses the sacred Emerald Buddha. Arrive early to beat the crowds and dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered).

Cost: 500 THB (~$15)

Hours: 8:30am-3:30pm daily

How to get there: Take the Chao Phraya Express Boat to Tha Chang Pier (N9)

2. Chatuchak Weekend Market

One of the world's largest markets with over 15,000 stalls selling everything from clothing to antiques. Go early for the best deals and bring cash.

When: Sat-Sun 9am-6pm

Tip: Section 26 has great local food

3. Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn)

Iconic riverside temple best visited at sunset when it's beautifully illuminated. Climb the steep steps for panoramic views of the Chao Phraya River.

Cost: 100 THB (~$3)

4. Khao San Road

The famous backpacker street with cheap hostels, street food, and vibrant nightlife. Even if you're not staying here, it's worth experiencing the energy.

Budget stay: Hostels from $5/night

5. Chinatown (Yaowarat)

Best visited in the evening when the street food stalls come alive. Don't miss the seafood restaurants and bird's nest soup shops.

Must-try: Shark fin soup (from ethical vendors), mango sticky rice

Budget Travel Tips

Getting Around

Skip taxis and use the BTS Skytrain or MRT subway. For short distances, tuk-tuks are fun but negotiate the price first (should be about 50-100 THB for most rides).

Best value: Rabbit Card for BTS (20% cheaper than single tickets)

Affordable Accommodation

Khao San Road is famous for backpackers but can be noisy. Consider nearby areas like Rambuttri or Silom for better value. Hostels start at $5/night, hotels at $20.

Recommended: Lub d Bangkok Silom, The Yard Hostel

Eating Cheap

Street food is delicious and safe to eat. Look for busy stalls with high turnover. Meals cost 40-100 THB ($1.20-$3).

Best areas: Chinatown, Sukhumvit Soi 38, Or Tor Kor Market

Food Recommendations

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pad Thai: Try Thip Samai (known as Pad Thai Pratu Pi)
  • Som Tam (Papaya Salad): Best from street vendors
  • Mango Sticky Rice: Available at most markets
  • Tom Yum Goong: Spicy shrimp soup at P'Aor
  • Khao Soi: Northern Thai curry noodles at Khao Soi Khun Yai

Best Street Food Areas

Chinatown (Yaowarat): Come hungry for seafood and noodle dishes

Sukhumvit Soi 38: Great for late-night eats

Or Tor Kor Market: High-quality produce and cooked foods

Local Dining Etiquette

It's common to share dishes family-style. Don't stick your fork in your mouth - use it to push food onto your spoon. Never leave your chopsticks upright in a bowl.

One Day Itinerary

8:00 AM

Breakfast at a Local Market

Start your day like a Thai with jok (rice porridge) or khao tom (boiled rice soup) at Or Tor Kor Market near Chatuchak.

9:30 AM

Grand Palace & Wat Pho

Visit these iconic sites early to avoid the worst crowds. Don't miss the 46-meter reclining Buddha at Wat Pho.

12:30 PM

Lunch at Tha Maharaj

Riverside dining complex with great views and authentic Thai food at reasonable prices.

2:00 PM

Wat Arun

Take the ferry across the river to this stunning temple. Climb to the top for panoramic views.

5:00 PM

Chinatown Street Food

Explore Yaowarat Road as the street food stalls set up for evening service.

8:00 PM

Rooftop Drinks

End your day at Octave Rooftop Lounge for affordable cocktails with amazing city views.

Essential Information

Language

Thai is the official language. English is widely understood in tourist areas. Learn basic phrases like "Hello" (Sawasdee) and "Thank you" (Khop khun).

Currency

Thai Baht (THB). $1 â 35 THB. ATMs are everywhere but charge 220 THB fee per withdrawal.

Electricity

220V, 50Hz. Type A, B, C plugs. Bring a universal adapter.

Transportation

BTS Skytrain and MRT subway are most efficient. Taxis are cheap but often stuck in traffic.

Safety

Generally safe but watch for pickpockets in crowded areas. Beware of tuk-tuk scams offering "cheap tours".

Best Time to Visit

Nov-Feb (cool season). Avoid Apr (hottest) and Sep-Oct (rainiest).

Cultural Do's & Don'ts

Do's

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples (cover shoulders and knees)
  • Remove your shoes when entering homes or temples
  • Use the "wai" greeting (palms together in prayer position)
  • Show respect to Buddha images

Don'ts

  • Touch people's heads or point your feet at people/Buddha images
  • Raise your voice or show anger in public
  • Criticize the monarchy (illegal in Thailand)
  • Take Buddha images out of the country without permission

Day Trips from Bangkok

Ayutthaya

Ancient capital with impressive ruins, 1.5 hours by train.

Cost: ~300 THB roundtrip

Damnoen Saduak Floating Market

Colorful market where vendors sell from boats, 1.5 hours by bus.

Tip: Go early to avoid crowds

Maeklong Railway Market

Market set up on active train tracks, combined with Damnoen Saduak.

Train times: 8:30, 11:10, 14:30, 17:40
