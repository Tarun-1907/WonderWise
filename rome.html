Rome, Italy

Eternal City
20°C, Sunny
Budget: $$
Timezone: GMT+1

Walk through 2,000 years of history in this open-air museum of a city.

Top 5 Things to Do

1. Colosseum

Iconic Roman amphitheater that once held 50,000 spectators. Book a guided tour to skip lines and access underground areas.

Cost: €16 combo ticket with Roman Forum

Best Time: First entry at 8:30am or last entry 1 hour before closing

2. Vatican City

Visit St. Peter's Basilica (free) and the Vatican Museums (book ahead) including the Sistine Chapel.

Cost: €17 for Vatican Museums (€21 with audio guide)

Best Time: Wednesday mornings (Papal Audience) or late afternoons

3. Pantheon

Best-preserved ancient Roman temple, now a church with the world's largest unreinforced concrete dome.

Cost: Free (was free until recently, may have small fee now)

Best Time: Early morning or rainy days (see the oculus effect)

4. Trevi Fountain

Baroque masterpiece where tossing a coin ensures your return to Rome. Visit at night for magical lighting.

Cost: Free (coin toss optional)

Best Time: Dawn or after midnight to avoid crowds

5. Roman Forum & Palatine Hill

Walk through the ruins of ancient Rome's political and social center, with great city views from Palatine.

Cost: Included in Colosseum ticket

Best Time: Late afternoon for golden light on ruins

Must-Try Roman Food

Cacio e Pepe

Simple but perfect Roman pasta with pecorino cheese and black pepper.

Where to try: Roscioli or Tonnarello in Trastevere

Price: €10-15

Supplì

Roman street food - fried rice balls with mozzarella center (like arancini but Roman style).

Where to try: Supplizio near Campo de' Fiori

Price: €2-3 each

Maritozzo

Sweet cream-filled bun, a traditional Roman breakfast pastry.

Where to try: Regoli Pasticceria near Termini

Price: €3-4

Budget Tips

Free First Sundays

Many state museums (including Colosseum) are free first Sunday of each month (expect crowds).

Drink from Nasoni

Rome's free drinking fountains provide excellent water - bring a refillable bottle.

Roma Pass

€32 for 48 hours includes transport and free/discounted entry to 1-2 sites.

Eat Away from Tourist Spots

Walk a few blocks from major sights for better food at half the price.

Sample 4-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Ancient Rome

  • Morning: Colosseum (book 9am entry)
  • Afternoon: Roman Forum & Palatine Hill
  • Evening: Dinner in Monti neighborhood

Day 2: Vatican & Trastevere

  • Morning: Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel
  • Afternoon: St. Peter's Basilica (climb dome for views)
  • Evening: Explore Trastevere and dinner

Day 3: Baroque Rome

  • Morning: Pantheon & Piazza Navona
  • Afternoon: Trevi Fountain & Spanish Steps
  • Evening: Walk through Campo de' Fiori night market

Day 4: Hidden Gems

  • Option 1: Borghese Gallery (book ahead) and Villa Borghese park
  • Option 2: Appian Way bike tour and catacombs
  • Evening: Sunset from Gianicolo Hill

Essential Information

Visa Requirements

Schengen visa rules apply. Many nationalities get 90 days visa-free.

Language

Italian is official language but English widely spoken in tourist areas.

Currency

Euro (€). Credit cards widely accepted but carry some cash for small purchases.

Electricity

230V, 50Hz. Type F/L plugs (European standard). Bring adapter if needed.

Transportation

Metro (2 lines), buses, trams. €1.50 single ticket valid 100 minutes. Walk where possible!

Health & Safety

Watch for pickpockets in crowds. Tap water safe to drink. Emergency number: 112.

FAQs

When is the best time to visit Rome?

April-May and September-October for pleasant weather. July-August is hot and crowded.

Do I need to book tickets in advance?

Essential for Colosseum and Vatican Museums (book at least 1-2 weeks ahead in peak season).

Is Rome safe for solo travelers?

Generally very safe, just beware of pickpockets in tourist areas and on public transport.

What's the dress code for churches?

Cover shoulders and knees for St. Peter's and other churches (carry a scarf).
