Tokyo, Japan

Capital City
18°C, Clear
Budget: $$
Timezone: GMT+9

Where cutting-edge technology meets ancient traditions in the world's largest metropolis.

Top 5 Things to Do

1. Shibuya Crossing

The world's busiest pedestrian crossing. Best view from Starbucks in Tsutaya building.

Cost: Free

Best time: Evening when neon lights are on

2. Senso-ji Temple

Tokyo's oldest temple in Asakusa. Walk through Nakamise shopping street for souvenirs.

Cost: Free (donations welcome)

Best time: Early morning to avoid crowds

3. Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden

Beautiful park blending Japanese, English and French garden styles. Perfect for cherry blossom viewing.

Cost: 500 JPY (~$3.50)

Best time: Spring for cherry blossoms or autumn for fall colors

4. TeamLab Planets

Immersive digital art museum where you walk barefoot through stunning interactive installations.

Cost: 3,800 JPY (~$26)

Best time: Weekday mornings (book tickets in advance)

5. Tsukiji Outer Market

Food paradise with fresh sushi, seafood bowls and Japanese snacks (inner wholesale market moved).

Cost: Free to enter (food purchases extra)

Best time: Early morning for freshest seafood

Must-Try Tokyo Food

Sushi

Fresh nigiri at Tsukiji outer market or conveyor belt sushi for budget options.

Where to try: Sushi Dai (Tsukiji) or Genki Sushi (Shibuya)

Price: 1,000-3,000 JPY (~$7-20) per person

Ramen

Rich pork broth or soy-based noodles - each shop has its specialty.

Where to try: Ichiran (chain) or Afuri (yuzu-flavored broth)

Price: 800-1,500 JPY (~$5.50-10) per bowl

Monjayaki

Tokyo's answer to okonomiyaki - runny savory pancake cooked on hotplate.

Where to try: Monja Street in Tsukishima

Price: 1,000-2,000 JPY (~$7-14) per portion

Budget Tips

Get a Suica/Pasmo Card

Reloadable IC card for trains/buses (cheaper than single tickets) and can be used at convenience stores.

Eat at Department Store Basements

Depachika food halls have discounted prepared foods in the evening (after 7pm).

Free Observation Decks

Skip Tokyo Skytree - free views from Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building or Shibuya Sky.

Convenience Store Meals

7-11, FamilyMart and Lawson offer surprisingly good and cheap meals (try onigiri!).

Sample 5-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Central Tokyo

  • Morning: Meiji Shrine & Harajuku (Takeshita Street)
  • Afternoon: Shibuya Crossing & Hachiko Statue
  • Evening: Shinjuku Golden Gai or Omoide Yokocho

Day 2: Traditional Tokyo

  • Morning: Senso-ji Temple & Nakamise Street
  • Afternoon: Ueno Park & Museums

Day 3: Modern Tokyo

  • Morning: Tsukiji Outer Market
  • Afternoon: TeamLab Planets & Odaiba
  • Evening: Roppongi Hills night views

Day 4: Day Trip Options

  • Option 1: Hakone (onsens & Mt. Fuji views)
  • Option 2: Nikko (temples & nature)
  • Option 3: Kamakura (Great Buddha & beaches)

  • Morning: Shimokitazawa (vintage shopping)
  • Afternoon: Akihabara (electronics & anime)
  • Evening: Ginza (high-end shopping & dining)

Essential Information

Visa Requirements

Many nationalities get 90 days visa-free. Check MOFA website for your country's requirements.

Language

Japanese. English signage in tourist areas but not widely spoken. Learn basic phrases.

Currency

Japanese Yen (¥). Cash still preferred at many places. 7-11 ATMs accept foreign cards.

Electricity

100V, 50/60Hz. Type A/B plugs (two flat pins). US devices usually work without adapter.

Transportation

Excellent subway system. Get Suica/Pasmo IC card. Consider JR Pass if traveling beyond Tokyo.

Health & Safety

Extremely safe. Tap water drinkable. No tipping. Emergency number: 110 (police) or 119 (ambulance).

FAQs

When is the best time to visit Tokyo?

March-May for cherry blossoms or October-November for pleasant weather. Avoid Golden Week (late April-early May).

Is Tokyo expensive?

Accommodation can be pricey but food/transport are reasonable. Budget travelers can manage on ¥8,000-10,000/day.

Do I need to speak Japanese?

Not essential but helpful. Many restaurants have English menus or picture menus. Google Translate works well.

What's the etiquette for temples/shrines?

Bow slightly before entering, wash hands at purification fountain, don't photograph during prayers, and toss coins in offering box.
