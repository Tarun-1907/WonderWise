The city that never sleeps offers world-class museums, Broadway shows, and iconic landmarks.

Top 5 Things to Do

1. Central Park

843-acre green oasis in the middle of Manhattan. Rent a bike or take a guided walking tour.

Free activities: People watching, Shakespeare in the Park (summer)

Best time: Early morning for peaceful walks

2. Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island

Iconic symbol of freedom and immigration museum. Reserve crown access months in advance.

Cost: $24.50 ferry ticket (includes both islands)

Tip: First ferry of the day has shortest lines

3. Metropolitan Museum of Art

One of the world's greatest art museums with collections spanning 5,000 years.

Cost: Pay-what-you-wish for NY state residents, $30 suggested for others

Best time: Weekday mornings

4. Times Square

The bustling heart of NYC with giant digital billboards and Broadway theaters.

Cost: Free (TKTS booth for discounted show tickets)

Best time: Evening for the full neon experience

5. High Line

Elevated park built on a historic freight rail line with great city views.

Cost: Free

Best time: Weekday mornings or sunset

Must-Try NYC Food

NY-Style Pizza

Thin, foldable slices with perfect crust. Skip the chains for local joints.

Where to try: Joe's Pizza (Greenwich Village) or Di Fara (Brooklyn)

Price: $3-5 per slice

Bagels with Lox

NY bagels are legendary - try them with cream cheese and smoked salmon.

Where to try: Russ & Daughters or Ess-a-Bagel

Price: $10-15

Pastrami Sandwich

Pile of perfectly spiced, tender meat on rye bread with mustard.

Where to try: Katz's Delicatessen (Lower East Side)

Price: $25 (huge portion)

Budget Tips

Use the Subway

$2.90 per ride or get unlimited MetroCard for longer stays. Avoid taxis.

Free Museum Days

Many museums have pay-what-you-wish hours (MoMA: Friday evenings).

Cheap Eats

Food trucks, dollar pizza slices, and Chinatown restaurants offer great value.

CityPASS

Save 40% on top attractions like Empire State Building and 9/11 Museum.

Sample 5-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Midtown Icons

  • Morning: Times Square & Broadway theaters
  • Afternoon: Top of the Rock observation deck
  • Evening: Broadway show (lottery for cheap tickets)

Day 2: Lower Manhattan

  • Morning: Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island
  • Afternoon: 9/11 Memorial & Financial District
  • Evening: Dinner in Chinatown/Little Italy

Day 3: Central Park & Museums

  • Morning: Central Park walk
  • Afternoon: Metropolitan Museum of Art
  • Evening: Museum of Modern Art (free Friday nights)

Day 4: Brooklyn & DUMBO

  • Morning: Walk across Brooklyn Bridge
  • Afternoon: Brooklyn Heights & DUMBO
  • Evening: Williamsburg nightlife

Day 5: Uptown & Harlem

  • Morning: American Museum of Natural History
  • Afternoon: Harlem soul food lunch
  • Evening: Jazz club in Harlem or Greenwich Village

Essential Information

Visa Requirements

ESTA required for visa waiver countries. Others need visitor visa.

Language

English. Spanish widely spoken in some neighborhoods.

Currency

US Dollar ($). Credit cards widely accepted but carry some cash.

Electricity

120V, 60Hz. Type A/B plugs (two flat pins). No adapter needed for US devices.

Transportation

24/7 subway system. Unlimited MetroCard best for stays over 3 days.

Health & Safety

Generally safe but be alert in crowds. Tap water drinkable. Emergency: 911.

FAQs

When is the best time to visit NYC?

April-June and September-October for pleasant weather. December for holiday magic.

How much should I budget per day?

Budget travelers: $100-150/day. Mid-range: $200-300. Luxury: $400+.

Is the subway safe at night?

Generally yes, especially on busy lines. Use well-lit stations and sit near conductor.

Do I need to tip?

Yes! 15-20% at restaurants, $1-2 per drink at bars, $2-5 for hotel staff.
