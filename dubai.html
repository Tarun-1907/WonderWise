Dubai, UAE

Futuristic City
35Â°C, Sunny
Budget: $$$
Timezone: GMT+4

Futuristic skyline meets traditional souks in this Middle Eastern metropolis.

Top 5 Things to Do

1. Burj Khalifa

World's tallest building with observation decks on 124th and 148th floors. Book in advance for sunset slots.

Cost: From 149 AED (~$40) for 124th floor

Best Time: Sunset for spectacular views

2. Dubai Mall & Fountain Show

World's largest shopping mall with aquarium, VR park, and stunning fountain shows every 30 minutes after dark.

Cost: Free (attractions extra)

Best Time: Evening for fountain shows

3. Desert Safari

Dune bashing, camel rides, and Bedouin-style camp with dinner under the stars.

Cost: 150-300 AED (~$40-80)

Best Time: October-April (cooler months)

4. Palm Jumeirah

Artificial island with luxury hotels, Aquaventure waterpark, and stunning views from The View at The Palm.

Cost: 100 AED (~$27) for The View observation deck

Best Time: Morning for waterpark, evening for views

5. Old Dubai & Souks

Explore historic Al Fahidi district and cross Dubai Creek by abra to visit gold and spice souks.

Cost: 1 AED (~$0.27) for abra ride

Best Time: Morning before it gets too hot

Must-Try Dubai Food

Shawarma

Thinly sliced marinated meat with garlic sauce in Arabic bread - Dubai's favorite street food.

Where to try: Al Mallah (Satwa) or any local cafeteria

Price: 8-15 AED (~$2-4)

Machboos

National dish of UAE - spiced rice with meat (usually chicken or lamb) and dried lemon.

Where to try: Local Emirati restaurants like Al Fanar

Price: 35-60 AED (~$10-16)

Luqaimat

Sweet fried dumplings drizzled with date syrup - a popular Emirati dessert.

Where to try: Arabian Tea House in Al Fahidi

Price: 25-35 AED (~$7-10)

Budget Tips

Use the Metro

Dubai's metro is clean, efficient and much cheaper than taxis. Day pass is 22 AED (~$6).

Visit Free Attractions

Many top sights are free: Dubai Fountain, Jumeirah Beach, Souks, Dubai Marina walk.

Eat at Local Cafeterias

Skip expensive hotel restaurants for authentic, affordable meals at local eateries.

Look for Combo Tickets

Attractions like Burj Khalifa + Dubai Aquarium often have discounted combo tickets.

Sample 4-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Modern Dubai

  • Morning: Burj Khalifa At The Top
  • Afternoon: Dubai Mall & Aquarium
  • Evening: Dubai Fountain show & dinner in Downtown

Day 2: Desert & Old Dubai

  • Morning: Al Fahidi Historic District
  • Afternoon: Abra ride to spice and gold souks
  • Evening: Desert safari with dinner

Day 3: Palm & Beaches

  • Morning: Aquaventure Waterpark or The View at The Palm
  • Afternoon: Jumeirah Beach or Kite Beach
  • Evening: Dinner at Dubai Marina

Day 4: Day Trip or Shopping

  • Option 1: Day trip to Abu Dhabi (Sheikh Zayed Mosque)
  • Option 2: Mall of Emirates & Ski Dubai
  • Evening: Global Village (seasonal) or La Mer beachfront

Essential Information

Visa Requirements

Many nationalities get 30-day visa on arrival. Check requirements based on your passport.

Language

Arabic is official language but English widely spoken. Most signs are bilingual.

Currency

UAE Dirham (AED). $1 â 3.67 AED (fixed rate). Credit cards widely accepted.

Electricity

220V, 50Hz. Type G plugs (British standard). Bring adapter if needed.

Transportation

Excellent metro, buses, and taxis. Careem/Uber available. Consider Nol card for public transport.

Health & Safety

Very safe city. Tap water technically drinkable but most prefer bottled. Emergency number: 999.

FAQs

What should I wear in Dubai?

Modest clothing recommended (cover shoulders/knees) in public areas. Swimwear fine at beaches/resorts.

When is the best time to visit?

November-March for pleasant weather (20-30Â°C). Avoid summer (May-Sep) when temperatures exceed 40Â°C.

Is Dubai expensive?

Can be, but budget options exist. Expect similar prices to major European cities for dining/attractions.

Can I drink alcohol in Dubai?

Yes, in licensed venues (hotels, bars). Public intoxication is illegal. No alcohol during Ramadan daytime.
