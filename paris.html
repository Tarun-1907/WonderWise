Paris, France

Capital City
15°C, Partly Cloudy
Budget: $$$
Timezone: GMT+1

The City of Light needs no introduction - world-class museums, iconic landmarks, and unparalleled cuisine await.

Top 5 Things to Do

1. Eiffel Tower

Paris's most iconic landmark. For the best free view, go to Trocadéro Gardens across the river.

Cost: €26.80 to top (stairs cheaper)

Tip: Book tickets online to skip lines

2. Louvre Museum

World's largest art museum. Don't try to see everything - focus on a few key works like Mona Lisa.

Cost: €17 (free 1st Sat evening/month)

Tip: Enter through Carrousel du Louvre to avoid main pyramid lines

3. Notre-Dame Cathedral

Gothic masterpiece currently being restored after fire. Still impressive to see from outside.

Cost: Free (donations welcome)

Tip: Visit nearby Sainte-Chapelle for stunning stained glass

4. Montmartre & Sacré-Cœur

Charming artsy neighborhood with panoramic city views from the basilica steps.

Cost: Free (€6 to climb dome)

Tip: Go early to avoid crowds and sketch artists

5. Seine River Cruise

See Paris's landmarks beautifully illuminated at night from the water.

Cost: €15-30 depending on company

Tip: Bring your own wine for cheaper Batobus rides

Must-Try Parisian Food

Croissants & Pain au Chocolat

Flaky, buttery perfection from a proper boulangerie (not touristy spots near landmarks).

Where to try: Du Pain et des Idées (10th arr.) or any local bakery

Price: €1-2 each

Steak Frites

Classic French bistro dish - perfectly cooked steak with golden fries.

Where to try: Le Relais de l'Entrecôte (they only serve this)

Price: €25-35 including salad and seconds

Macarons

Delicate French sandwich cookies in endless flavors.

Where to try: Pierre Hermé or Ladurée (for the classic experience)

Price: €2-3 each

Budget Tips

Museum Pass

Paris Museum Pass gives skip-the-line access to 50+ museums (2-day pass €52).

Free First Sundays

Many museums are free first Sunday of the month (expect crowds).

Picnic by the Seine

Buy cheese, bread and wine from local shops for a fraction of restaurant prices.

Walk or Use Metro

Paris is very walkable. For longer distances, metro tickets are €1.90 each.

Essential Information

Visa Requirements

Schengen visa rules apply. Many nationalities get 90 days visa-free.

Language

French. Learn basic phrases - even "Bonjour" goes a long way.

Currency

Euro (€). Credit cards widely accepted but carry some cash.

Electricity

230V, 50Hz. Type E plugs (two round pins). Bring adapter if needed.

Transportation

Excellent metro system. Consider carnet of 10 tickets (€16.90) or Navigo pass for longer stays.

Health & Safety

Beware pickpockets in tourist areas. Tap water drinkable. Emergency number: 112.

FAQs

When is the best time to visit Paris?

April-June and September-October for pleasant weather. August is hot and many locals are away.

Do Parisians really hate tourists?

No, but they appreciate when visitors try speaking French and follow basic etiquette.

Is Paris safe?

Generally very safe, but beware pickpockets in tourist areas and on metro.

Do I need to tip in Paris?

Service charge is included, but rounding up or leaving 5-10% extra is appreciated for good service.
