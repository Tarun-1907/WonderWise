Lima, Peru

Gastronomic Capital
20°C, Cloudy
Budget: $
Timezone: GMT-5

Gateway to Machu Picchu with incredible seafood and colonial architecture.

Top 5 Things to Do

1. Miraflores District

Upscale coastal neighborhood with parks, shopping, and paragliding over cliffs.

Paragliding: ~$60 for 10-minute flight

2. Historic Center of Lima

UNESCO World Heritage site with stunning colonial architecture including Plaza Mayor and the Cathedral of Lima.

Cathedral Entrance: ~$5

3. Larco Museum

World-class museum showcasing 5,000 years of Peruvian history with an impressive pre-Columbian art collection.

Admission: ~$12

4. Barranco District

Bohemian neighborhood with colorful houses, street art, and Lima's best nightlife.

Free to explore!

5. Magic Water Circuit

World-record holding water fountain park with spectacular light shows at night.

Entrance: ~$4

Budget Tips

Affordable Eats

Try local markets like Mercado de Surquillo for cheap and authentic Peruvian meals (~$3-5).

Free Walking Tours

Many companies offer free walking tours (tip-based) of historic Lima and Barranco district.

Public Transportation

Use the Metropolitano bus system (~$0.50 per ride) instead of taxis for getting around.

Food & Drink

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: Peru's national dish of raw fish cured in citrus
  • Lomo Saltado: Stir-fried beef with onions, tomatoes and fries
  • Pisco Sour: Peru's signature cocktail made with pisco brandy
  • Anticuchos: Grilled beef heart skewers (tastier than it sounds!)

Best Restaurants

  • Central (#1 in Latin America): High-end tasting menu (~$150)
  • La Mar: Excellent mid-range cevicheria (~$30)
  • Tanta: Affordable chain with all Peruvian classics (~$15)

Sample Itinerary

3 Days in Lima

Day 1: Historic Lima

  • Morning: Walking tour of Historic Center (Plaza Mayor, San Francisco Monastery)
  • Lunch: Traditional Peruvian at Cordano
  • Afternoon: Larco Museum
  • Evening: Dinner in Barranco, bar hopping

Day 2: Modern Lima

  • Morning: Paragliding in Miraflores
  • Lunch: Ceviche at La Mar
  • Afternoon: Shopping at Larcomar mall
  • Evening: Magic Water Circuit show

Day 3: Day Trip

  • Option 1: Pachacamac archaeological site
  • Option 2: Ballestas Islands wildlife tour
  • Evening: Farewell dinner at Central or Maido

Essential Information

Language

Spanish is official language. English spoken in tourist areas.

Currency

Peruvian Sol (PEN). $1 ≈ 3.7 PEN. USD sometimes accepted.

Electricity

220V/60Hz. Type A and C plugs (bring adapter).

Safety

Generally safe but beware pickpockets. Avoid certain areas at night.
