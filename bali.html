Bali, Indonesia

Island Paradise
30°C, Sunny
Budget: $
Timezone: GMT+8

Tropical beaches, lush jungles, and rich Hindu culture make Bali a perfect getaway.

Top 5 Things to Do

1. Uluwatu Temple

Clifftop temple with stunning ocean views and famous Kecak fire dance at sunset.

Cost: 50,000 IDR (~$3.50)

Best Time: Late afternoon for sunset

2. Tegallalang Rice Terraces

Iconic emerald-green rice paddies with beautiful layered landscapes.

Cost: 20,000 IDR (~$1.40) donation

Best Time: Early morning to avoid crowds

3. Mount Batur Sunrise Trek

Hike this active volcano before dawn for breathtaking sunrise views.

Cost: 300,000-500,000 IDR (~$20-35) with guide

Best Time: Dry season (April-October)

4. Sacred Monkey Forest

Explore this jungle sanctuary with hundreds of playful long-tailed macaques.

Cost: 80,000 IDR (~$5.50)

Best Time: Morning when monkeys are most active

5. Nusa Penida Day Trip

Visit this stunning neighboring island with dramatic cliffs and crystal waters.

Cost: 600,000-1,000,000 IDR (~$40-70) for tour

Best Time: April-November when seas are calm

Must-Try Balinese Food

Nasi Goreng

Indonesian fried rice with sweet soy sauce, often served with fried egg and satay.

Where to try: Warungs (local eateries) everywhere

Price: 15,000-30,000 IDR (~$1-2)

Babi Guling

Succulent roast pork with crispy skin, a Balinese specialty.

Where to try: Ibu Oka in Ubud (most famous)

Price: 50,000-100,000 IDR (~$3.50-7)

Lawar

Traditional mix containing vegetables, coconut, minced meat and rich spices.

Where to try: Local warungs, best in Gianyar region

Price: 20,000-40,000 IDR (~$1.40-3)

Budget Tips

Eat Local

Warungs (local eateries) serve delicious meals for $1-3 versus $10+ at tourist restaurants.

Rent a Scooter

At ~50,000 IDR/day (~$3.50), it's the cheapest way to get around (if you're experienced).

Stay in Guesthouses

Basic but clean rooms with fan start around 150,000 IDR (~$10) in less touristy areas.

Negotiate Everything

From taxis to souvenirs, polite bargaining is expected and can save you 30-50%.

Sample 5-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival & Uluwatu

  • Arrive in Denpasar, transfer to hotel
  • Afternoon: Relax at Padang Padang Beach
  • Evening: Uluwatu Temple & Kecak dance at sunset

Day 2: Ubud Cultural Day

  • Morning: Sacred Monkey Forest
  • Midday: Tegallalang Rice Terraces
  • Afternoon: Ubud Palace & Art Market
  • Evening: Traditional Balinese dance performance

Day 3: Mount Batur Adventure

  • 2am: Start sunrise trek up Mount Batur
  • Sunrise: Enjoy views with breakfast cooked by volcanic steam
  • Late morning: Visit Tirta Empul water temple
  • Afternoon: Relax at Tegalalang Spa

Day 4: Nusa Penida Excursion

  • Early ferry to Nusa Penida
  • Visit Kelingking Beach, Angel's Billabong, Broken Beach
  • Snorkel at Manta Point (seasonal)
  • Evening return to Bali mainland

Day 5: Relaxation & Departure

  • Morning: Balinese cooking class
  • Afternoon: Last-minute shopping in Seminyak
  • Evening: Transfer to airport

Essential Information

Visa Requirements

Free 30-day visa on arrival for most nationalities. Extensions possible at immigration offices.

Language

Bahasa Indonesia is official language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas. Learn basic phrases like "Terima kasih" (thank you).

Currency

Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). $1 ≈ 14,000 IDR. ATMs widely available. Credit cards accepted at hotels/resorts.

Electricity

230V, 50Hz. Type C/F plugs (European standard). Bring universal adapter.

Transportation

Blue Bird taxis are reliable. Gojek/Grab for ride-hailing. Scooter rentals everywhere (wear helmet!).

Health & Safety

Drink bottled water. Mosquito repellent essential. Petty theft occurs - secure valuables.

FAQs

When is the best time to visit Bali?

April-October (dry season) is ideal. July-August is peak season with higher prices. November-March is rainy but still pleasant with fewer crowds.

Is Bali safe for solo female travelers?

Generally yes, especially in tourist areas. Take normal precautions at night and with scooters. Dress modestly when visiting temples.

Do I need to tip in Bali?

Tipping isn't expected but appreciated for good service. Round up taxis, 10% at restaurants is generous. Some places add service charge.

What should I pack for Bali?

Light clothing, swimwear, sunscreen, mosquito repellent, sandals, modest clothing for temples, universal adapter, waterproof phone case.
